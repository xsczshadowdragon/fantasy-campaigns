Fantasy Campaigns Guide

Fantasy campaigns is a turn-based strategy game where players obtain territories, manage a civilization, build economies & armies, and compete with others to complete objectives and achieve victory.

Tutorial

Creating a character

The first thing a player will do after joining the server is create their character. To do so, choose an ancestry, a combat class, and a civilization to join. In the wiki is more information to help you decide how to make your character.

Turn-based gameplay

The game is played by turns. Each civilization has a turn and every player in that civilization will perform their actions. Players should enable notifications for the channel so that they will know when it is their turn. The host of the campaign will be available to assist players if need be. Any player who is consistently absent will be ejected from the campaign but their character will be saved. Every turn civilizations generates money and resources.

Moving armies

AFter an army is trained, it will wait in it's respective territory until you choose to invade another territory. Invading a territory takes 1 round. In other words, after all the other civilizations have had their turn, your invasion will commence.

Civilizations

A civilization is comprised of it's ancestry (examples: humans, elves, dwarves), it's name, a banner, a leader, and it's members. Leaders are elected through votes or assigned by the campaign host.

Roleplay

Roleplaying is not required but it is encouraged. Roleplaying adds to the ambiance of the game and helps players stay in character and focused on the game.

Resources

Resources are: Money, Metal, Wood, Stone, Food, and Crystals. Each resource is used in it's own way for building new structures, crafting tools/items/weapons/armor, buying services or other materials, trading, and other objective related purposes. Civilizations accumulate resources by it's buildings and also by player deposits.

Population

Civilizations need population for soldiers and workers. Building housing increases a settlements population.

Armies

Armies are trained by military buildings comprised of infantry, ranged infantry, cavalry, siege equipment.

Special units

Special units are used for tactical purposes. A few examples of special units are scouts, assassins, diplomats, priests, and guards.

Territories

Territories are claimed by the civilization that first builds a home base there. Once a territory has been claimed, it's natural resources go to the civilization which has claimed it. Other civilizations can still put structures and armies in that territory. Other civilizations can also claim your territory by defeating all the military there and if remains unchallenged for 10 turns will obtain that territory.

Battle

Battles use a combination of unit stats and dice rolls to determine outcome. A common unit has an attack and hit points. To attack an enemy army, you must first know their location. Once you have scouted the enemy, you can then initiate an attack. If you move your army into a hostile region and the opponent has a scout in that army, they will be aware of your presence and your units.

Attack, hit points

A unit’s attack is based on it’s dice. For example a basic sword infantry will roll a 1d4. If a player rolls a 3 and the enemy unit has 3 hp, the unit will perish.

Actions

Player actions are submitted in their respective civilization's actions channel. Here is the list of possible actions (some of these actions are exclusive to civilization leaders): buy, sell, trade, build, destroy, train unit, attack, hide unit, camp unit, recruit player, promote player, demote player, exile player, engage diplomacy, offer gift, offer region, offer money, offer neutral treaty, declare war, offer peace treaty.

Switching civilizations

Once a player joins a civilization they have a few days to switch if they change their mind. However a player can only switch civilizations once per month.

Cancelling actions

When a player performs an action they have have 5 minutes to send a request to the GM to cancel it. If another player has already performed an action after yours, you cannot cancel your action.

Traitors and Spying

If a player is caught giving information about any civ to another civ, the leader of that civilization can choose to fine them, imprison them (unable to play for 7 days) or exile them from the civilization. That player will also receive a traitor role for 1 month’s duration.
