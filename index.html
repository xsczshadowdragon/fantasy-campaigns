Attributes

Base Attributes

Strength: Melee damage
Constitution: Health points
Dexterity: Chance to hit, chance to critical hit, chance to evade
Intelligence: Problem solving, crafting bonus,
Wisdom: Spell power, spell resistance
Charisma: Relationships

Multipliers

For every certain amount of points you put into a stat, it increases stats related to that attribute. You only receive a bonus multiplier when you reach the required amount of points for that stat. For example look at this template of a starter character at level 1.

Strength: +1 x0.1
Constitution: +1 x0.1
Dexterity: +1 x0.1
Intelligence: +1 x0.1
Wisdom: +1 x0.1
Charisma: +1 x0.1

All of their stats are below +4 so they only have a x0.1 multiplier. However, we will update some stats and then see what happens.

Strength: +4 x0.3
Constitution: +2 x0.1
Dexterity: +3 x0.1
Intelligence: +4 x0.3
Wisdom: +2 x0.1
Charisma: +1 x0.1

Strength and intelligence received a x0.3 multipier because it reached +4. The trend continues onward each time, but as you level up, the requirement gets higher.

Character Creation

The first thing a player needs to do is create a character. When a player creates a character, the first thing they choose is an ancestry. In Legend and Myth, the player does not choose a class for their character in the beginning. The player develops their character's class over time through choices in game. Class development is done through NPC trainers. For example, in order to pursue the mage class archetype, one must seek out a mage trainer. In the beginning of the game, the player can choose only one trainer. However, at a higher level the player can then go on to be what is considered "multi-class". In other words, if you want to multi-class as a mage/rogue you would reach the required level and then seek out a rogue trainer. After the player chooses an ancestry they then choose their character's name and appearance.

Damage Types and Conditions

Physical damage types

Physical damage types are caused by non-magical weapons.

- Hack: Swords, axes, some polearms, some traps
- Blunt: Maces, hammers, and martial arts
- Piercing: Most polearms, arrows, thrown weapons, some swords, some hammers, bullets
- Explosive: Bombs, traps, some bullets

Magical damage types

Magical damage types are from spells and some skills.

- Holy: This is mostly effective against forbidden enemy types
- Forbidden: Necromancy, dark magic, shadow magic, blood magic
- Elemental: Fire, earth, water, air
- Physical: From spells like push, crush, and choke
- Mental: From mind magic

Conditions

These are conditions usually inflicted from melee combat and sometimes magic.

- Bleeding
- Blindness
- Paralyzed
- Muted: Cannot cast magic
- Exhaustion: Decreased energy for skills
- Wounded: Decreased total health
- Drained: Decreased mana
- Diseased: Spreads to allies and is hard to cure
- Accursed: If not remedied by a priest, the character will turn to the forbidden
- Vampirism: Causes by the bite of a vampire, and will convert the character over time
- Lycanthropy: Like vampirism, only it turns the character into a werewolf
Inventory System

A player holds items in storage containers that they have on their character. For example, starting players have a small bag which can hold 10 items. If the player wants to increase their inventory space, they must purchase additional storage containers. The player can have a maximum of 4 storage containers. Mounts can also have storage containers and hold items in addition to the player's storage containers. Players that choose the merchant job can purchase a caravan which acts as a mount and a large storage unit as well.
Jobs

Jobs are an economic part of the game. The purpose of a job is for a player to develop skills which later can help them in crafting or even combat related skills. Each job has has repetitive tasks they can do to level up in their job. At certain levels, the player earns abilities or skills which can help them in game. Once the player reaches a specific level in their job, they can choose a second job if they want.

Magic

Forbidden magic

Ancient dark magic which is forbidden to use across all the empires. There are some who have chosen to seek the path of evil and study the tomes which grant the user terrible power.

Holy magic

Magic used for healing and protection. However, it can be very powerful when used against the undead and forsaken mages.

Arcane magic

Mysterious and complex. Arcane magic is the preference of most wizards. It is considered to be magic of space and time.

Spirit magic

Shamans and ritualists tap into the spirit world to learn powerful spells from those who have died long ago. Spirit magic is not always from evil sources. Some spirit magic calls upon saints of old.

Mind magic

Usually those using this magic are up to no good. Spells meant to manipulate a creature's mind.

Rune magic

The dwarves long ago crafted magic spells using the stones and gems they found in the deep mountain mines. It requires a special kind of soul to study it. Even the greatest wizards have been puzzled by it's essence.

Nature magic

The forces of nature are wild and raw. Venture into the wildnerness and perhaps one can tap into it's source. At the highest mountain peak, in the depth of the ocean, in the shade of the forests.

Dragon magic

It is a peculiar kind of magic which draws upon many other kinds of magic in combination. The problem with it is that it's not easy to learn magic from a dragon that isn't fond of your presence.

Elemental magic

Fire, earth, air, and water. Not all mages can master all of the elements, for it has nearly never been done but by a few.

Factions

World Dragons
Ancestry: Dragons
Morality: Neutral
Personality: Proud, greedy
Enemies: Giants of Old, Keepers of the Crystals
Allies: None

Creatures that stood the test of time. They are known for hoarding gems, gold and silver. Dragons are found all over the world. They thrive independently. Dragons and dwarves have always had a strong rivalry because they compete for the rare stones hiding in the mountains.

Urlog's Empire
Ancestry: Ogres
Morality: Evil
Personality: Primal
Enemies: Giants of Old
Allies: None

Though primal as they are, the ogres have established one of the largest and vastest empires. The bulk of the ogre horde is in the plains and deserts of Ginivid. The ogre-king of the ogric empire is Urlog, known as the buzzard-king for his fascination with bones. Long ago Urlog hunted many giants and used their bones to fashion his fortress. This gave him a gruesome and cruel reputation.

Keepers of the Crystals
Ancestry: Naga
Morality: Neutral
Personality: Mystical
Enemies: Forsaken, World Dragons
Allies: None

Mysterious and powerful serpents. They can communicate with mortals telepathically and use powerful mind magics. They mostly live in the waters, but they can go on land for long durations of time. They are known to hunt dragons for their hearts, but their mind magic does not work well on them. They are the most feared enemy in all the lands and seas.

The Red Claw
Ancestry: Kobold
Morality: Evil
Personality: Primal
Enemies: Goblins
Allies: None

Red-skinned creatures that look similar to goblins, except they are known for their hairy manes and variety of masks. They are known for their culture of rituals and shamanism. Only sometimes will you see ogres and kobolds getting along. Kobolds sometimes work for dragons in acquiring gems and metals.

The Forsaken
Ancestry: Any
Morality: Evil
Personality: Sadistic
Enemies: All
Allies: None

The forsaken are those who follow the way of Eneem, the god of corruption. They are considered to be possessed. The longer they worship Eneem, the stronger their bodies become. The most powerful of the forsaken cannot be slain by ordinary weapons.

Giants of Old
Ancestry: Giant
Morality: Good
Personality: Stoic
Enemies: Urlog's Empire, Dragons
Allies: None

Most of their kind are gone, destroyed by the ogre horde and dragons. They have taken up hold in isolated places away from danger. Their lesser kin the half-giants however exist in larger numbers and they're known for having a grudge against the ogres and dragons alike.

Weapons

Weapons come in many types. There are one handed weapons, two handed weapons, ranged weapons, throwing weapons, and special weapons like explosives and instruments. Instruments are not used for melee attacks, but rather are needed for lore magic.

Damage types

The types of weapon damage are hack, blunt, piercing, and explosive. Some weapons have combined damage types, like pikes, fencing swords, or hammer-picks.

Quality and Condition

Equipment comes in many different forms of quality or condition. Usually this quality is determined by whomever has crafted it. Depending on the skill of the crafter, the formula, and the materials used.

Dual wielding, two handed weaponry, archery & throwing

Small weapons require a minimum of +4 dexterity to equip in your other-hand. Larger weapons wll require more. Most two handed weapons require a minimum of +4 strength. To use ranged weapons like bows, crossbows, and throwing weapons, a character needs +4 dexterity.

Swords

Swords are one of the most common weapons used by fighters and warriors. They come in a variety of types such as long swords, short swords, katanas, and fencing swords. Some swords can do both hacking and piercing damage. Short swords are a bit quicker and lighter in general. There are two handed swords also.

Daggers

Daggers are light, fast, and easy to conceal. They generally do piercing damage, though some do hacking also.

Axes

Axes do the most hacking damage of all the weapons. They tend to be a little heavier, but not as heavy as hammers. They can inflict bleeding conditions.

Hammers

Hammers cause crushing damage and can inflict crippling conditions. One-handed hammers are the heaviest of their kind.

Polearms

Polearms inflict the most damage compared to all the other weapons, but they are the heaviest type. Most polearms are two-handed weapons.

Throwing weapons

There are many kinds of throwing weapons, usually if it's light enough, it can be thrown. There are also some special skills which allow characters to throw normal weapons.

Bows

Bows come as shortbows, longbows, and crossbows. However, crossbows are not affected by attributes that govern efficiency with standard bows.

Guns

Guns can cause crushing damage, piercing damage, explosive damage, as well as inflict a variety of conditions. They are very effective but expensive to use.

Armor

Clothes

Clothes are used by mage and rogue types which do not use armor. They do not provide much protection, however they can be enchanted such as mage robes or special clothing for sneaking.

Light armor

Light armor comes in a variety of materials such as leather, hide, and chainmail. However not all chainmail is considered to be light armor.

Heavy armor

Heavy armor is the toughest and heaviest. It is used by strongest of fighters and warriors. The most common types of heavy armor are bronze, iron, steel, and titanium.

Shields

Light shields

Light shields do not offer as much protection as heavy shields, but they still can offer protection to the user. Light shields are usually made of a combination of wood, leather, hide, and sometimes metal binding.

Heavy shields

Heavy shields can be as tall as a man. Heavy shields are not for the faint of heart as they require alot of stamina to use them. Most heavy shields are made of bronze, iron, steel, and titanium.
